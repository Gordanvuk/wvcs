It has been asked what sort of notes or prompts you should use in a presentation like this. My answer is as follows:
A transcript is usually hard to read "live".... notes or cards is usually easier. Last year, I remember someone commenting that card prompts were not necessary for such a short presentation... but if you're not used to presentation, you probably want some sort of aide memoire, as much as a "security blanket" as a real aide. In other words, use whatever you find useful, but remember it is a very short talk!

    Presentation Plan and Timings

    The aim is to do a presentation: the subject is just a vehicle for constructing and giving a presentation!

    The presentation has four elements:

    1. Paper details: author, title, publication details
    2. Context: a brief statement of the area of computer science, and general background to the paper
    3. Content: what the paper covers
    4. Evaluation: concluding comments on the merits and demerits of the paper

    Content review should outline the underlying theme(s) of the paper, and perhaps illustrate a small example of what is covered.

    Evaluation might be a qualitative assessment (advanced/trivial; easy read/unnecessarily complex etc), but should be supportable! Alternatively, it might be a statement of who should use the paper, and for what.

    As a rough guide, the paper details can be displayed; the context should take a minute at most; the content should take about 2 minutes, and the evaluation no more than a minute.
