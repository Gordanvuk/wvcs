LAST DAY FOR QUESTIONS: 19 JANUARY 2009

These are questions and answers about the CSW assessment for 2008-09.

Questions must be e-mailed to king@cs.york.ac.uk.

Questions will not be answered after Spr/2/Mon, Monday 19 January 2009.

Questions will always be answered on this page, not necessarily directly to the questioner.

Questions and their answers are given in reverse order of their answering. The dates are the dates that the answer is posted, not that when the question is asked. Questions will be made anonymous when transcribed here.

You may find it helpful to see questions that have been asked in previous years, when the rubrics have been very similar.

Questions are emphasised, answers not:

    Answer

/Spr/2/Mon

  • I presume that the lit review is not required to be just plain text i.e. pseudocode/equations/examples/algorithms etc are permitted.
    Is that correct?

    It certainly is correct: whatever text / diagrams / equations etc are needed to construct the review should be used!

     

  • I am doing a self defined project next year and so have to do research into that area to come up with the project. I was wondering if I could use the area of the project as the area for my literature review, or if the topic must come almost verbatim from the pages of the research groups in the department or the staff members.

    If I were answering back when you first asked the question, I'd probably have said: you must do what the assessment rubric says, ie 'from the research groups and staff in them'. But my answer now must be slightly more 'relaxed'!

    As long as you can clearly identify an area, and papers in that area, then that's fine.

     

  • I'm  currently sorting my CSW layout in latex and noted the footnote on p6  that advised the plain latex style is more compact. Does this mean we  can use the plain style? I was under the assumption that we had to use  the UoY layout but I"m often wrong on these things.

    No, you don't *have* to use any particular style. We are a very non-prescriptive department ... in general! :-)

    Actually, reading the footnote again, it could be clearer: it is not referring to the 'plain' TeX style, but the 'article' style for LaTeX (on which JLJ's project style is based).
     

Steve King
