Computer Science Writing (CSW)
Presentations

All students taking PR3 will make an unassessed presentation in Autumn week 5. 3rd year MEng/MMath students and MScNC students will do assessed presentations in the spring term. The remainder of this page applies to PR3 students: details for next term's presentations will be given later.

PR3 student presentations

The timetabled slots for presentations are given on the CSW webpage here. Note that the Tuesday slot starts at 3.15pm, and lasts only until 4.15, not as in the centrally published timetable. I have divided the other 2 hour slots into two: please be prompt for all sessions, as the timing is very tight. If you have problems with the timetable, please find a student in another session to swap with, and let me know by email of the changes.

Sign-up sheets for the various slots are posted outside my office (CS120K) / on the 3rd year noticeboard. Please sign up before Monday 9th at 11am: after this time, I will check for anyone who has not signed up, and allocate them to a session!

Each student will make a 5 minute presentation, using a set of PowerPoint (or other application) slides. They may also give the audience any material on paper they think would be useful for the audience to look at during the presentation (this is unlikely, but if you have a table or something that is too detailed to present as a slide.

The audience will be Steve King, a PhD student (demonstrator) and the other students in the group. So if you have decided to provide a handout, you will need to bring up to 11 copies of it.

Students will be expected to stay for the full session of their presentation slot and may ask questions of other students' presentations. Students are also welcome to attend presentation sessions when they are not presenting themselves, except for the 2.15 and 3.15 sessions on Thursday in G/045A, which is only large enough for the timetabled group.

Students doing PR3 will make their presentation on their project literature review. (All other students (MEng/MMath and MSc NC) will later make their presentation on the open assessment literature review that they have conducted.)

Students should email their PowerPoint (or other application) slides to Steve King king@cs.york.ac.uk at least 24 hours before their presentation slot, so we can be sure that they will run. The email should also state which session the slides are for.

The presentation should review one, or at most two, key papers from your literature review. It should cover:

  • topic of the paper/s - this can be your title slide
  • author/s and title of the paper/s brief description of what the paper/s are about, focussing on the part/s you are most interested in
  • some critique of the paper/s - can be positive or negative, what is most interesting/wrong etc with the paper/s (this is the meat of the presentation, so might be two slides
  • conclusion

Note that this structure suggests 4 or 5 slides. Remember that I suggested that you should not aim for much more than 1 slide per minute, so for a 5 minute talk, this is about all you need.

Feedback: all students giving presentations will receive written feedback afterwards. For PR3 students, it is likely to be week 6 before it can be collated and distributed.
