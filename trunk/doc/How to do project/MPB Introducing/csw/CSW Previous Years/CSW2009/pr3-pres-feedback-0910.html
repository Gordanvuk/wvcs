Unassessed CSW presentations, for PR3-ers

The presentations were, on the whole, interesting and fun to listen to!

On the positive side, almost all of the slides used were clear and well-designed. However, there were a very few cases where diagrams were rather small and hard to read. Almost all students spoke clearly and enthusiastically.

For such short presentations (the assessed ones next term are twice as long, at 10 minutes + questions), the judgement about what to put in and what to leave out is very important. Some people didn't leave space for their own critique of the paper(s), giving only a summary of the content: I think that was a wrong decision! It was also noticeable that many presentations finished significantly before the allotted 5-minute time had expired: what a waste! It is always worth practising a presentation in advance, to get the timing right, and you should remember the effect of adrenalin: you are likely to speak faster in a slightly stressed situation, so this should be taken into account when working out timings.
