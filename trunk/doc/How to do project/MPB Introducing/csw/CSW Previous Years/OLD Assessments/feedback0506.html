Assessment Feedback 2005-06

There were a lot of very good reviews, this year. There were fewer dire ones than last year, and a relatively low level of sloppy presentation.

Revised marking criteria this year allowed some people to score well because their presentation and referencing was good, rather than because they produced good reviews. These are reported in individual feedback.

A few people were still unable to follow the given structure, despite dire threats in lectures. The clearly delimited structure and requirements for each section were put there to help students present the review in a conventional way, recognising that most students have not had much experience in this area.

It is clear that a number of people have not spent much of the assumed 90 non-contact hours of CSW researching literature review. The lectures explained the need to present coherent argument, not precis, recommended students to study past projects, and gave annotated lists of the best projects for the various degrees in recent years.

Some people are still not taking note of how to use citation, despite a whole lecture on this.

The commonest problem overall was presenting precis not review. Many assessments had two or three successive precis, sometimes with personal commentary, but nothing that pulled together the points, coverage, or issues. A few exacerbated the problem by providing the review discussion in the conclusion (where it was penalised as new material).

A small number of students are in serious need to help (or hard work) on presentation and writing. These are noted in individual feedback. These students will have problems with open assessments and PR5 next year if they cannot write more clearly, correctly and concisely.
